About Me

I am currently in the Geographic Information Systems Advanced Diploma Part-time program at the British Columbia Institute of Technology (BCIT). I am projected to graduate by the summer of 2026.

I also have a BSc in geology from the University of British Columbia which I completed in 2021. Working as a geologist in northwestern BC in mineral exploration, I got some exposure to GIS in the form of data collection and mapping in the field using tools like QField, QGIS, and Garmin GPS devices. This is when I developed an interest in GIS and decided to pursue the diploma at BCIT. For the future, I look forward to applying the GIS skills I developed through schooling, mineral exploration, and my own projects in my career.

What I like most about GIS is the abstract nature of maps in how they represent real-world objects using symbols. I also quite enjoy cartography, the artistic process of designing maps, and automating workflows with things like Python.

Some of my personal interests include reading philosophy, walking, practicing yo-yo tricks, and watching movies.
